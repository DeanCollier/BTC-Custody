Bitcoin Custody

Now that we have covered what bitcoin is, lets go over the different ways an individual can custody their bitcoin! In order to understand the different custody options, we need to first understand what a bitcoin address and what a bitcoin wallet are.

Address

  1. Private Key - a private key represents a randomly generated 256 bit number that is unique (see below for all posibilities)
  2. Public Key - a public key is an encrypted version of your private key that you can share with others to recieve bitcoin

Wallet

  1. Key Storage - a wallet stores your private key, the private key is needed to sign transactions that you make
  2. Blockchain Interaction - a wallet uses your bitcoin address to display how much bitcoin you own
  3. Transactions - wallets allow you to send and recieve bitcoin

Private Key Storage

Now that we know about our private key and wallets, how should be make sure that our private key doesn't get stolen or lost?

There are a few different options for wallets, each of which stores your private key with different levels of security.

Exchange Wallet:

  • Keeping your btc on an exchange can be convenient if you are a trader or want immediate access to selling your btc.
  • In this case, the exchange itself holds the private key, this means your keys are susceptible to exchange hacks.
  • This may be appropriate for some people with smaller amounts of btc.

Desktop/Mobile Wallet:

  • This is a wallet installed on your personal computer or smartphone.
  • Your private key is stored on that device, so it is in your possession
  • This may be better in the sense that your own your private key, but is still susceptible to attacks by hackers accessing your device.

Hardware Wallet

  • This is a device that stores your private key and is not connected to the internet.
  • This is the safest of the three key storage options.
  • Your keys are in your full custody and it is your responsiblity to safegaurd them.
